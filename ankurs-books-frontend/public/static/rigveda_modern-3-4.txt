HYMN I. Agni.
URL: https://sacred-texts.com/hin/rigveda/rv03001.htm
--------------------------------------------------
1. Agni, you who make things strong, you've made me the Soma priest, to worship in the assembly.
 You shine for the gods, I set the press-stones. I work hard; be joyful in yourself, Agni.

2. We turned eastward for the ritual; may the hymn help it. With wood and offerings, they honor Agni.
 From heaven, the councils of the wise have learned it: for the living and the strong they seek advancement.

3. The prudent one, whose heart is pure, brought prosperity, allied by birth to Heaven and Earth in kinship.
 The gods discovered Agni in the midst of waters, through the sisters' labor, beautiful and strong.

4. Him, Blessed One, the Seven strong floods augmented, him white at birth and red when mighty.
 As mother mares run to their newborns, so at his birth the gods wondered at Agni.

5. Spreading with radiant limbs across the land, purifying his power with wise purifications,
 Clothed in light, the life of waters, he spreads his high and perfect glories.

6. He sought the Mighty Ones, the unconsuming, the unimpaired, not clothed and yet not naked.
 Then they, ancient and young, who dwell together, Seven sounding Rivers, as one germ received him.

7. His altars, taking every form, are scattered where sweet waters flow, at the spring of abundance;
 There stood the milch-kine with full udders, and both paired Mighty Mothers of the Wondrous.

8. Carefully cherished, Son of Strength, you shine, assuming lasting and radiant beauty.
 Full streams of fatness and sweet juice descend, where the Mighty One grows strong by wisdom.

9. From birth he knew even his father’s breast, he set his voices and streams in motion;
 Knew him who moves with blessed friends in secret, with the young damsels of heaven. He did not hide.

10. He nourished the infant of the sire and maker: alone the babe sucked many a teeming breast.
 Guard, for the Bright and Strong, the friendly spouses, for men and bound to him in kinship.

11. The Mighty One expanded in boundless space; many a glorious flood gave strength to Agni.
 Friend of the household, within the lap of Order lay Agni, in the Sister Rivers’ service.

12. As keen supporter where great waters gather, light-shedder whom the brood rejoice

HYMN II. Agni.
URL: https://sacred-texts.com/hin/rigveda/rv03002.htm
--------------------------------------------------
To Agni, Vaiśvānara, who strengthens Holy Law, we offer our praise like oil made pure.
With thoughtful insight, human priests bring him near, our Herald from of old, as an axe forms a car.

He made the heaven and earth resplendent by his birth: Child of two Mothers, he was meet to be implored,
Agni, oblation-bearer, gracious, ever-young, infallible, rich in radiant light, the guest of men.

Within the range of their surpassing power, by might, the Gods created Agni with inventive thought.
I, eager to win strength, address him, like a steed, resplendent with his brilliance, with his ample light.

Eager to gain, we crave from him the friendly God strength confident, choiceworthy meet to be extolled:
The Bhṛgus' bounty, willing, strong with sages' lore, even Agni shining forth with light that comes from heaven.

For happiness, men, having trimmed the sacred grass, set Agni glorious for his strength before them here;
Yea, with raised ladles, him bright, dear to all the Gods, perfecting aims of works, Rudra of solemn rites.

Around thy dwelling-place, O brightly-shining Priest, are men at sacrifice, whose sacred grass is trimmed.
Wishing to do thee service, Agni, they are there, desirous of thy friendship grant them store of wealth.

He hath filled heaven and earth and the great realm of light, when at his birth the skilful held him in their hold.
He like a horse is led forth to the sacrifice Sage, graciously inclined, that he may win us strength.

Honour the oblation-bearer, him who knows fair rites, serve ye the Household Friend who knows all things that be.
He drives the chariot of the lofty ordinance: Agni most active, is the great High Priest of Gods.

They who are free from death, fain for him, purified three splendours of the mighty Agni, circling all.
To man, for his enjoyment, one of these they gave: the other two have passed into the sister sphere.

Man’s sacrificial food has sharpened like an axe, for brightness, him the Sage of men, the people’s Lord,
Busied with sacred rites he mounts and he descends. He hath laid down his vital germ within these worlds.

He stirs with life in wombs diss

1. To him who shines afar, Vaiśvānara, shall bards give precious things that he may go on certain paths:
 For Agni the Immortal serves the Deities, and therefore never breaks their everlasting laws.

2. He, wondrous envoy, goes between the earth and heaven, firm seated as the Herald, great High Priest of men.
 He compasses with rays the lofty dwelling-place, Agni, sent forward by the Gods, enriched with prayer.

3. Sages shall glorify Agni with earnest thoughts, ensign of sacrifice, who fills the synod full:
 In whom the singers have stored up their holy acts to him the worshipper looks for joy and happiness.

4. The Sire of sacrifice, great God of holy bards, Agni, the measure and the symbol of the priests,
 Hath entered heaven and earth that show in varied form: the Sage whom many love rejoices in his might.

5. Bright Agni with the bright car, Lord of green domains, Vaiśvānara dweller in the floods, who finds the light,
 Pervading, swift and wild, encompassed round with powers, him very glorious have the Gods established here.

6. Agni, together with the Gods and Manu's folk by thought extending sacrifice in varied form,
 Goes, car-borne, to and fro with those who crown each rite, the fleet, the Household Friend, who turns the curse aside.

7. Sing, Agni, for long life to us and noble sons: teem thou with plenty, shine upon us store of food.
 Increase the great man's strength, thou ever-vigilant: thou, longing for the Gods, knowest their hymns full well.

8. The Mighty One, Lord of the people and their guest, the leader of their thoughts, devoted Friend of priests,
 Our solemn rites' announcer, Jātavedas, men with worship ever praise, with urgings for their weal.

 Let us with pure laudations in his house approach the high laws of the nourisher of multitudes.

10. I celebrate thy glories, O Vaiśvānara, wherewith thou, O farsighted God, hast found the light.

HYMN IV Āprīs.
URL: https://sacred-texts.com/hin/rigveda/rv03004.htm
--------------------------------------------------
1. Be friendly with each kindled log of fuel, with every flash bestow the boon of riches.
Bring the Gods, O God, to our worship: serve well, as Friend, your friends, O Agni.

2. Agni, whom daily Varuṇa and Mitra the Gods bring three times a day to this our worship,
Enrich with meath our service that dwells with holy oil, that offers honor.

3. The thought that brings every boon proceeds first to worship the Libation Priest,
That we may greet the Strong One with our homage. Urged, may he bring the Gods, best Sacrificer.

4. On high your way to sacrifice was made ready; the radiant flames went upward to the regions.
Full in the midst of heaven the Libation Priest is seated: may we the sacred grass where Gods may rest them.

5. Claiming in mind the seven priests' burnt offerings, inciting all, they came in settled order.
To this our sacrifice approach the many who show in hero beauty at assemblies.

6. Night and Dawn, lauded, hither come together, both smiling, different are their forms in color,
That Varuṇa and Mitra may accept us, and Indra, girded by Maruts, with his glory.

7. I crave the grace of heaven’s two chief Invokers: the seven swift steeds joy in their wonted manner.
These speak of truth, praising the eternal truth, thinking on Order as the guards of Order.

8. May Bhāratī with all her Sisters, Iḷā in harmony with the Gods, with mortals Agni,
Sarasvatī with all her kindred Rivers, come to this grass, Three Goddesses, and seat them.

9. Well pleased with us do you, O God, O Tvaṣṭar, give ready issue to our procreative vigor,
From which springs the hero, powerful, skilled in action, lover of Gods, adjuster of the press-stones.

10. Send to the Gods the offering, Lord of Forests; and let the Immolator, Agni, dress it.
He as the truer Priest shall offer worship, for the Gods’ generations well he knows.

11. Come to us, O Agni, duly kindled, together with the potent Gods and Indra.
On this our grass sit Aditi, happy Mother, and may our Hail delight the Immortals.

HYMN V. Agni.
URL: https://sacred-texts.com/hin/rigveda/rv03005.htm
--------------------------------------------------
1. Agni, who shines against the dawns, is awakened. The holy singer who precedes the sages.
With widespread radiance, kindled by the devout, the priest has opened both doors of darkness.

2. Agni has grown mighty through praise, to be adored with hymns of those who sing its praises.
Loving the varied aspects of holy order at the dawn’s first glow, it shines as an envoy.

3. Agni has been established among men’s homes, fulfilling the law, friend, the root of waters.
Loved and adored, it has ascended high, the singer, the object of our invocations.

4. Agni is Mitra when properly kindled, Mitra as priest, Varuṇa, Jātavedas;
Mitra as active minister, and house-friend, Mitra of flowing rivers and mountains.

5. The Earth guards his dear lofty place, guarding the course of Sūrya with might.
Guarding the Seven-headed in the center, guarding the sublime enjoyment of the gods.

6. The skilled God who knows all forms of knowledge created for himself a fair form, fit for worship.
This Agni protects the Soma’s skin, the Bird’s place rich in fatness, with care that never stops.

7. Agni eagerly enters the fat shrine, giving easy access. Radiant, pure, sublime, and purifying,
It again and again renovates its mothers.

8. Born suddenly, by plants it grows to greatness, when tender shoots increase it with holy oil.
Like waters lovely when they rush downward, may Agni protect us in its parents’ lap.

9. Praised, the strong one shines forth with kindling fuel to the earth’s center, to the height of heaven.
May Agni, friend, adorable Mātariśvan, as envoy bring the gods to our worship.

10. Best of all luminaries, lofty Agni supports the height of heaven,
When, far from Bhṛgus, Mātariśvan kindles the oblation-bearer where he lies in secret.

11. As holy food, Agni gives wealth in cattle, lasting, rich in wonders to your invoker.
May we be born a son and spreading offspring. Agni, may this be your gracious will to our words.

HYMN VII.
URL: https://sacred-texts.com/hin/rigveda/rv03007.htm
--------------------------------------------------
1. The seven tones rose from the white food and traveled between the pair of Mothers.
Both Motherly Parents come together to grant us long life; they hasten forward.

2. The One dwelling in heaven has Mares and Milchmaids; He came to the Goddesses who bring sweet treasure.
To you, safe resting in the seat of Order, the Cow alone proceeds on her way.

3. Wise Master, Wealthy Finder of Riches, He mounted those who can be guided with care.
He, dark-haired, manifold with varied aspects, made them burst forth from their food, the brushwood.

4. Strength-bearing streams carry him eternally, to support the mighty work of Tvaṣṭar.
He, flashing in his home with all his parts, entered both the worlds as they were single.

5. They know the red Bull's blessing and are joyful under the flaming Lord's rule:
They who give shine from heaven with fair radiance, whose lofty song like Iḷā must be honored.

6. By tradition from the ancient sages, they brought great strength from the two mighty Parents,
To where the singer's Bull, the night's dispeller, after his proper law has grown stronger.

7. Seven holy singers guard with five Adhvaryus the Bird's beloved firmly-settled station.
The willing Bulls, untouched by old age, rejoice them: as Gods themselves the ways of Gods they follow.

8. I crave the grace of heaven’s two chief Invokers: the seven swift steeds joy in their customary manner.
These speak of truth, praising the Eternal Truth, thinking on Order as the guards of Order.

9. Many seek the Great Horse as a stallion; the reins obey the Lord of varied color.
O Heavenly Priest, most pleasant, full of wisdom, bring the great Gods to us, and Earth and Heaven.

10. Rich Lord, the Mornings have shone in splendor, fair-radiant, fair-speaking, worshipped with all viands,
Yea, with the glory of the earth, O Agni. Forgive us, for our sins committed, even though small.

11. As holy food, Agni, to your invoker, give wealth in cattle, lasting, rich in wonders.
May we be born a son, and spreading offspring, Agni, may this be your gracious will to us.

HYMN VIII Sacrificial Post.
URL: https://sacred-texts.com/hin/rigveda/rv03008.htm
--------------------------------------------------
GOD-serving men, O Sovereign of the Forest, anoint you with heavenly mead at sacrifice.

Grant us wealth when you stand upright, as when resting on your Mother’s breast.

Set up to the east of the fire, accepting prayers that do not waste, rich in heroes.

Drive far from us poverty and famine, lift yourself to bring us great good fortune.

Lord of the Forest, raise yourself to the highest point on earth.

Give splendor, fixed and measured well, to him who brings the sacrifice.

Well-robed, enveloped, you have come, the young one: your glory grows greater as you spring to life.

Thoughtful in mind and devoted to the gods, sages of high intelligence uphold you.

Risen up, you increase in the council of men. With song, the wise and skilled consecrate you. His voice, the god-devoted singer utters.

Ye whom religious men have firmly planted; you, Sovereign of the Forest, whom the axe has shaped—

Let those divine stakes standing here be glad to grant us wealth and many children.

O men who lift the ladles up, these hewn and planted in the ground,

Bringing a blessing to the field, they shall bear our precious gift to the gods.

Adityas, Rudras, Vasus, careful leaders, Earth, Heaven, and Prthivi and Air’s mid-region,

Accordant deities shall bless our worship and make our sacrifice’s banner lofty.

Like swans flying in a long line, the pillars have come to us in brilliant colors.

They, lifted high by sages, eastward, go forth as gods to the gods’ dwellings.

Those stakes on the earth, with rings that deck them, seem to the eye like the horns of horned creatures;

Or, as raised by priests in invocation, may they assist us in the rush to battle.

Lord of the Wood, rise with a hundred branches. May we rise with a thousand branches under you,

Whom this hatchet, well sharpened for great happiness, has brought before us.

HYMN IX.
URL: https://sacred-texts.com/hin/rigveda/rv03009.htm
--------------------------------------------------
1. WE as your friends have chosen you, mortals, to be our helper,
 The Waters' child, the blessed one, resplendent and victorious, unmatched.

2. Since you delighted in the woods and went to your mother streams,
 Not to be scorned, Agni, is that return of yours when you come back here.

3. Over fragrant smoke, you prevailed, and thus you are benevolent.
 Some go before, and others sit around you, those who have your friendship.

4. Him who had passed beyond his enemies, beyond endless pursuit, 
 Him the unerring ones, observant, found in floods, lying like a lion in his den.

5. Him who wandered at his own free will, Agni hidden from our sight,
 Him Mātariśvan brought to us from far away, produced by friction, from the gods.

6. O Bearer of offerings, mortals received you from the gods,
 While you, the friend of man, guard each sacrifice with your own might, Youngest One.

7. Amidst your wonders, this is good, indeed, clear to the simple,
 When gathered around you, Agni, lie the herds where you are kindled in the morning.

8. Offer to him who knows proper rites, who burns with purifying light,
 Swift envoy, active, ancient, and adorable: attend to the god carefully.

9. Three times a hundred gods and three times a thousand, and three times ten and nine have worshiped Agni,
 For him spread sacred grass, with oil anointed him, and established him as priest and sacrificer.

1. AGNI, God, Lord of all humanity, mortals ignite you
 At the ritual with wisdom.
 2 They praise you in solemn rites, Agni, as both priest and minister,
 Shine in your own home as the protector of the law.
 3 He who honors you with fuel, knower of all life,
 He, Agni, wins heroic strength and prosperity.
 4 Banner of offerings, Agni, come to us with the gods,
 Adorned by seven priests, to the giver of gifts.
 5 Offer your best, your lofty speech to Agni, the invoking priest,
 The one who ordains and brings the light of song.
 6 May these hymns make Agni grow, so that he may rise in praise,
 To mighty strength and great wealth, beautiful to behold.
 7 Best sacrificer, bring the gods, O Agni, to the devout man:
 A joyful priest, drive your glory far from our enemies.
 8 As such, O Purifier, shine on us with heroic, glorious might:
 Be closest friend to those who praise you, for their benefit.
 9 So, watchful, skilled in sacred hymns, holy singers, kindly honor you.
 Oblation-carrier, immortal, strength-giver.

 He knows the ritual in constant practice.

 Agni comes close to us with the thought.
 3 Banner of sacrifice from ancient times, Agni well knows with his thoughts
 To help this man achieve his goal and hope.
 4 Agni, illustrious from long ago, the Son of Strength who knows all life,
 The Gods have made him their priest.
 5 Unerring is Agni, he who leads the clans of men,
 A swift chariot and always new.
 6 Strength of the Gods that no one can harm, subduing all his enemies,
 Agni is the mightiest in fame.
 7 By offering sacred food to him, the mortal worshipper gains a home.
 A place from him whose light makes pure.
 8 From Agni, through our hymns, may we gain all things that bring happiness,
 Singers of him who knows all life.
 9 O Agni, in our deeds of might may we obtain all precious things:
 All the Gods are centered in you.

HYMN XII. Indra-Agni.
URL: https://sacred-texts.com/hin/rigveda/rv03012.htm
--------------------------------------------------
1. Moved, Indra-Agni, by our hymn, come to the juice, the precious dew:
Drink ye thereof, impelled by song.

2. O Indra-Agni, with the man who lauds you comes the wakening rite:
So drink ye both this juice assured.

3. Through force of sacrifice I choose Indra-Agni who love the wise:
With Soma let these sate them here.

4. Indra and Agni I invoke, joint-victors, bounteous, unsubdued,
Foe-slayers, best to win the spoil.

5. Indra and Agni, singers skilled in melody hymn you, bringing lauds:
I choose you for the sacred food.

6. Indra and Agni, ye cast down the ninety forts which Dāsas held,
Together, with one mighty deed.

7. To Indra-Agni reverent thoughts go forward from the holy task
Along the path of sacred Law.

8. O Indra-Agni, powers are yours, and dwellings and delightful food
Good is your readiness to act.

9. Indra and Agni, in your deeds of might ye deck heaven's lucid realms:
Famed is that hero strength of yours.

1. Drink the finest Soma juice, O Vāyu, at our sacred rites:

2. Come, drawn by teams with your hundred helpers, with Indra,

5. Come, Indra-Vāyu, to the man who offers gifts:
Come hitherward.

7. Here, O Indra-Vāyu, be your journey unyoke your steeds,
Here for your Soma drink.

HYMN L. Bṛhaspati.
URL: https://sacred-texts.com/hin/rigveda/rv04050.htm
--------------------------------------------------
1. To Him who with might supports Earth’s ends, who sits in a threefold seat, Bṛhaspati, with thunder,
Ancient sages, deep-thinkers, holy singers, ancient ones have set Him before them.

2. Wild and swift, in well-defined ways, they moved around us, Bṛhaspati.
Preserve Bṛhaspati, let His stall remain unharmed, this company’s dwelling, ever-moving birthplace.

3. From afar, those who love the Eternal Law sit down, Bṛhaspati.
For You, they dug springs from mountains, which murmur as they pour sweet streams.

4. Bṛhaspati, when first born from mighty splendor in the highest heaven,
Strong with His sevenfold mouth, with the roar of thunder, with seven rays, He dispelled the darkness.

5. With the loud-shouting choir, He sang His praises, with thunder, He destroyed the obstructive Vala.
Bṛhaspati, with thunder, drove out the cattle, the lowing cows ready for offerings.

6. We serve with sacrifices, gifts, and homage, the Steer of all the Gods, the Father.
May we be lords of wealth, with noble offspring and a store of heroes.

7. That Hero, by might and power, has made Himself Lord over all His enemies’ possessions.
He cherishes Bṛhaspati well, adorns and worships Him as the foremost sharer.

8. In His own house, He dwells in peace and comfort; to Him, ever-holy food flows richly.
To Him, the people pay homage freely—The King with Brahman’s precedence.

9. Unopposed, He rules over His subjects’ riches and the enemy’s people.
The Gods uphold that King with Their protection, helping Brahman when He seeks favor.

10. Indra, Bṛhaspati, rainers of treasure, rejoicing at this sacrifice, drink the Soma.
Let the abundant drops sink deep within You: grant us riches with full store of heroes.

11. Bṛhaspati and Indra, may this be Your benevolence to us—Make us prosperous.
Help our holy thoughts, awaken our spirit: weaken the hatred of our foe and rivals.

HYMN LI. Dawn.
URL: https://sacred-texts.com/hin/rigveda/rv04051.htm
--------------------------------------------------
1. From the eastward darkness, this most abundant splendid light has mounted.
Now truly, the far-refulgent Mornings, daughters of heaven, grant welfare to the people.
2. The richly-coloured Dawns have mounted eastward, like pillars planted at our sacrifices,
And, flushing far, splendid and purifying, they open the doors of the darkness fold.
3. This day, the wealthy Mornings urge generous givers to present their treasures.
Let stingy traders sleep unawakened in the dark depths around them.
4. O Goddesses, is this your chariot? Ask me, ancient today, or new, you Mornings,
With rich Dawns, seeking wealth Navagva, Dasa-gva Aṅgira, the seven-toned singer?
5. With horses harnessed by eternal Order, Goddesses, swiftly traverse the worlds,
Awakening, O Dawns, the sleeping and all that lives—man, bird, and beast—to motion.
6. Among these, which is the oldest, and through whom do they fix the Rbhus' regulations?
When the splendid Dawns go forth for splendor, they are not known apart, alike, unwearying.
7. Blessed were these Dawns of old, shining with aid, true to the truth that springs from holy Order;
With whom the toiling worshipper, praising and lauding, soon attains riches.
8. All at once, from the eastward, they travel together, from one place spreading in the same manner.
Awakening, from the seat of holy Order, the divine Dawns come near like herds of cattle.
9. Thus they go forth with unchanged colors, these Mornings, similar, in the same way,
Concealing the giant might of darkness with radiant bodies bright and pure and shining.
10. O Goddesses, O heavens' radiant daughters, grant us wealth with a store of children.
As from our pleasant place of rest you awaken us, may we be masters of heroic vigor.
11. Skilled in the lore of sacrifice, daughters of heaven, radiant Dawns, I address you thus.
May we be glorious among the people. May heaven grant us this, and earth the goddess.

1. This Lady, giver of joy, after her sister shines brightly, daughter of the sky, has appeared.

HYMN LIII. Savitar.
URL: https://sacred-texts.com/hin/rigveda/rv04053.htm
--------------------------------------------------
1. To Savitar, the wise Asura God, we seek this great gift worthy of our choice,
With which he freely grants his worshiper protection. With his rays, the Great God has granted us this.

2. Nurturer of the sky, Lord of all life’s sustenance, the Sage dons his golden armor.

Clear-sighted, spreading far, filling the vast realm, Savitar has brought forth bliss that deserves praise.

3. He fills the heavens and earth with abundance; the God strengthens himself by singing the hymn.

Savitar stretches out his arms to cherish life, producing with his rays and calming all that moves.

4. Lighting all living beings, never to be deceived, Savitar, God, protects each holy ordinance.
He stretches out his arms to all the people of earth, and, with his laws observed, rules his mighty course.

5. Savitar thrice encircling with his might in the air, three regions, and the triple sphere of light,
Sets the three heavens in motion and the threefold earth, and willingly protects us with his triple law.

6. Most gracious God, who brings to life and lulls to rest, he who controls the world, what moves not and what moves,
May he grant us shelter—Savitar the God—tranquil life, with triple protection against distress.

7. With the year’s seasons has Savitar, God, come near; may he prosper our home, give food and noble sons.
May he invigorate us through the days and nights, and may he send us prosperity with offspring.

HYMN LIV. Savitar.
URL: https://sacred-texts.com/hin/rigveda/rv04054.htm
--------------------------------------------------
Now we must praise and honor Savitar, the God: At this time of the day, men should call upon him,
Who distributes wealth to Manu’s offspring, so that we may here enjoy excellent riches.

For you, at first, created for the holy gods the noblest portion, immortality;
Afterwards, as a gift to men, O Savitar, you opened existence, life following life.

If we, as we are, have offended the gods through lack of thought, weakness, or arrogance,
Forgive us our sins and make us free from wrongdoing, O Savitar, equally among gods and men.

None may hinder that power of Savitar, the God, by which he will sustain the universal world.
Whatever fair-fingered God brings forth on earth’s expanse or in the heights of heaven, that work of his stands firm.

To lofty hills you send those whom Indra leads, and give them fixed abodes with houses.
However far they may fly and separate, still, Savitar, they stand under your command.

May the libations poured to you three times each day, every day, O Savitar, bring us blessings.
May Indra, Heaven, Earth, Sindhu with the waters, Aditi with Ādityas, protect us.

HYMN LV. Viśvedevas.
URL: https://sacred-texts.com/hin/rigveda/rv04055.htm
--------------------------------------------------
1. Who among you, Vasus, protects? You, Heaven and Earth, and Aditi, guard us,
Varuṇa, Mitra, from the stronger mortal. Which of you at sacrifice gives comfort?

2. Those who praise the ancient laws with praise, when they shine forth without fail,
Have established them as eternal guides and shown holy thoughts.

3. May the Housewife Goddess, Aditi, and Sindhu, the Goddess Svasti, be my friend:
And may the unobstructed Night and Morning, day and night, protect us.

4. Aryaman, Varuṇa revealed the path, Agni, Lord of Strength, the way to well-being.
May Indra-Viṣṇu grant us their powerful defense and shelter.

5. I beseeched the favor of the Maruts, Parvata, Bhaga, the rescuer from man's troubles.
Mitra, let Mitra save us from woes sent by a friend.

6. Through these watery offerings, Goddesses, Heaven and Earth, with Ahibudhnya.
As if to win the sea, the Gharma-heaters open, as they approach, the rivers.

7. May Aditi, with the gods, defend us, save us, the ever-caring protector.
We do not stint the sacred food of Mitra and Varuṇa on Agni’s back.

8. Agni is the Sovereign Lord of wealth, Agni of great prosperity:
Grant us these gifts.

9. Hither, rich and pleasant Dawn, bring many things desired,
You who have ample wealth.

10. Then may Bhaga, Savitar, Varuṇa, Mitra, Aryaman, Indra, bring bounty to us.

HYMN LVI. Heaven and Earth.
URL: https://sacred-texts.com/hin/rigveda/rv04056.htm
--------------------------------------------------
1. MAY mighty Heaven and Earth, most fitting for honor, be present here with light and radiant glory;
 When, placing them apart, vast and extensive, the Steer roars loudly in far-reaching paths.

2. The Goddesses with Gods, holy with holy, the Two stand pouring out their rain, boundless:
 Faithful and without deceit, having Gods as children, leaders of sacrifice with brilliant glory.

3. Sure in the worlds he was a skilled Craftsman, he who produced these Two the Earth and Heaven.
 Wise, with his power he brought both realms, together spacious and deep, well-formed, unshaken.

4. O Heaven and Earth, with one accord promoting, with high protection as of Queens, our welfare,
 Far-reaching, universal, holy, guard us. May we, car-borne, through song be victorious always.

5. To both of you, O Heaven and Earth, we bring our lofty song of praise,
 Pure Ones! to glorify you both.

6. Ye sanctify each other’s form, by your own proper might ye rule,
 And from of old observe the Law.
7. Furthering and fulfilling, ye, O Mighty, perfect Mitra’s Law.
 Ye sit around our sacrifice.

 What sustains our cattle and horses. May He be kind to us.

 WITH meath and milk, make us happy. Grant us prosperity, ŚUNA and SĪRA.

HYMN LVIII. Ghṛta.
URL: https://sacred-texts.com/hin/rigveda/rv04058.htm
--------------------------------------------------
1. From the ocean rose the sweet wave: with the stalk it became Amṛta,
That which is holy oil’s secret title: but the Gods' tongue is truly Amṛta's core.

2. Let us declare aloud the name of Ghṛta, and at this sacrifice hold it high with reverence.
So may the Brahman hear the praise we utter. This was emitted by the Buffalo with four horns.

3. Four are its horns, three are the feet that carry it; its heads are two, its hands seven in number.
Bound with a triple bond roars the Steer; the mighty God has entered mortals.

4. That oil in triple form the Gods discovered and laid down within the Cow, hidden by Paṇis.
Indra created one form, Sūrya another: by their own power they formed the third from Vena.

5. From the deepest reservoir in countless channels flow these rivers that the foe cannot see.
I behold the streams of oil descending, and there is the Golden Reed among them.

6. Like rivers our libations flow together, purifying themselves in the inner heart and spirit.
The streams of holy oil pour swiftly downward like wild beasts fleeing before the bowman.

7. Like rushing down the rapids of a river, swifter than the wind are the vigorous currents flowing.
The streams of oil surge like a red courser bursting through the fences.

8. Like women gathered at a fair, fair to look on and gently smiling, they incline to Agni.
The streams of holy oil reach the fuel, and Jātavedas joyfully receives them.

9. As maidens adorn themselves with gay attire to join the bridal feast, I now behold them.
Where Soma flows and sacrifice is ready, there run the streams of holy oil.

10. Send to our eulogy a herd of cattle, bestowing upon us excellent possessions.
Bear to the Gods the sacrifice we offer, the streams of oil pure and full of sweetness.

11. The universe depends on your power and might within the sea, within the heart, within all life.
