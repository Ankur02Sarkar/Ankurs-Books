HYMN I. Agni
URL: https://sacred-texts.com/hin/rigveda/rv05001.htm
--------------------------------------------------
1. Agni is awakened by the people’s fuel to meet the Dawn, who comes like a milch-cow.
Like young trees sprouting high, his flames rise to the sky.

2. For the worship of the gods, the priest is awakened; at dawn, gracious Agni rises.
Kindled, his radiant power is revealed, and the great deity freed from darkness.

3. When he stirs his attendant line, pure Agni is anointed with his purity.
The gift that strengthens is then prepared, with tongues erect, he drinks it in.

4. The spirits of the pious gather to Agni, as all eyes turn to Sūrya.
He, when both Dawns of different hues bear him, rises at dawn as a strong white charger.

5. The noble One was born at the start of days, red in colour amid well-laid fuel.
Giving seven rich treasures in every house, Agni sits, the most skilled priest.

6. Agni has seated himself, the most skilled priest, on a fragrant spot, his mother’s breast.
Young, faithful, wise, preeminent over many, kindled among the folk who sustain them.

7. This excellent singer at sacrifices, Agni the priest, they honor with praise and homage.
Who spread out both worlds by Eternal Law, they anoint with oil, the strong steed that never fails.

8. He, worshipful House-Friend, in his home is worshiped, our own auspicious guest, praised by sages.
That strength the Bull with a thousand horns possesses. In might, O Agni, you excel others.

9. You swiftly pass by all others, Agni, for whom you appear most lovely,
Wonderfully fair, adorable, effulgent, the guest of men, the people’s darling.

10. To you, Most Youthful God! From near and far, the people bring their tribute.
Mark well the prayer of him who best extols you. Great, high, auspicious, Agni, is your shelter.

11. Ascend today your splendid chariot, O Agni, in splendor, with the Holy Ones around it.
Know the paths through the spacious region of mid-air, bring the Gods to feast on our offering.

12. To him adorable, wise, strong, and mighty, we have sung our praise and homage.
Gavisthira raised this far-reaching praise, like gold to heaven, to Agni.

But, when he lies upon the arm, the people see his unchanging face before them.
2. What child dost thou carry as handmaid, O Youthful One? The Consort-Queen bore him.
The unborn Babe grew through many seasons. I saw him born when his Mother bore him.
3. I saw him from afar, golden-toothed, bright-colored, hurling his weapons from his dwelling,
When I gave him Amṛta pure without mixture. How can the Indraless, the hymnless harm me?
4. I saw him moving from the place he dwells in, just like a herd, brilliantly shining.
These did not seize him: he had been born already. Those who were gray with age again become youthful.
5. Who separates my young bull from the cattle, those whose protector was truly not a stranger?
Let those whose hands have seized them free them. May he, observant, drive the herd to us.
6. Mid mortal men, godless have hidden the King of all who live, home of the people.
So may the prayers of Atri give him freedom. Reproached in turn be those who now reproach him.

As a kind friend with streams of milk, they blend you together, making wife and lord one-minded.

Men have established Agni as their own priest and serve him gladly for praise from those who live.

HYMN IV. Agni.
URL: https://sacred-texts.com/hin/rigveda/rv05004.htm
--------------------------------------------------
1. O Agni, King and Lord of wealth and treasures, in you I find joy at offerings.
May we gain the strength we seek and overcome the fierce attacks of mortals.

2. Agni, Eternal Father, bearer of offerings, fair to behold, far-reaching, far-refulgent,
From the well-kept household fire, you provide sustenance, and measure out abundant glory.

3. Sage of men, Lord of human races, pure and purifying Agni, balmed with butter,
Establish you, Omniscient, as our priest; among the gods, win things worth choosing.

4. Agni, enjoy this fuel with Iḷā, competing with Sūrya's beams in rivalry,
Enjoy, O Jātavedas, this offering, and bring the gods to taste it.

5. As dear House-Friend, welcome guest in the dwelling, to this sacrifice come thou who knowest.
And, Agni, scattering all assailants, bring to us the spoils of our foes.

6. Drive away the Dasyu with your weapon. As gaining vital power for your own body,
O Son of Strength, the gods you satisfy; in battle, save us, most heroic Agni.

7. May we, O Agni, with our praises adore you, and with our gifts, fair-beaming Purifier!
Send to us wealth containing all precious things: bestow upon us every sort of riches.

8. Son of Strength, Agni, dwelling in three regions, accept our sacrifice and oblation.
Among the gods, may we be counted pious; protect us with a triply-guarding shelter.

9. Over all woes and dangers, Jātavedas, bear us as in a boat across a river.
Praised with our homage even as Atri praised you, O Agni, be the guardian of our bodies.

10. As I, remembering you with grateful spirit, a mortal, call on you Immortal,
Vouchsafe us high renown, O Jātavedas, and may I be immortal by my children.

11. The pious man, O Jātavedas Agni, to whom you grant ample room and pleasure,
Gains abundant wealth with sons and horses, with heroes and with cattle for his well-being.

HYMN V. Āprīs.
URL: https://sacred-texts.com/hin/rigveda/rv05005.htm
--------------------------------------------------
1. To Agni, the well-enkindled, the flame, offer thick sacrificial oil.
2. He, Narasamha, never deceived, inspires this sacrifice:
   Sage is he, with sweets in hand.
3. Adored, O Agni, bring here Indra the Wonder-worker, the Friend,
   On a light car to help us.
4. Spread yourself out, soft as wool, what the holy hymns have sung to you.
   Bring gain to us, beautiful!
5. Open yourselves, divine doors, easy to access for our aid:
   Fill the sacrifice more and more.
6. Strongeners of vital power, mothers of eternal law,
   Morning and Night, we pray to you.
7. Come, glorified, the two celestial priests of man,
   Come to this sacrifice.
8. Iḷā, Sarasvati, Mahī, three Goddesses who bring us good,
   Sit harmlessly on the grass.
9. Rich in all plenty, Tvaṣṭā, come auspicious of your own accord,
   Help us in every sacrifice.
10. Vanaspati, wherever you know the Gods' mysterious names,
   Send our offerings there.
11. To Agni and Varuṇa, Indra, the Maruts, and the Gods,
   With Svāhā, bring the offering.

HYMN VI. Agni.
URL: https://sacred-texts.com/hin/rigveda/rv05006.htm
--------------------------------------------------
1. I honor Agni, the good lord, the place where cattle return:
Where swift horses seek it as home, and sturdy horses as home. Bring food to those who sing your praise.
2. We praise Agni as good, to whom the cows come in herds,
To whom swift-footed horses come, to whom our noble princes come. Bring food to those who sing your praise.
3. Agni, the god of all mankind, gives a horse to man.
Agni gives valuable gear for wealth, treasures he gives when pleased. Bring food to those who sing your praise.
4. God, Agni, let us light you up, rich in splendor, never fading,
So that this glorious fuel may send out its light by day for you. Bring food to those who sing your praise.
5. To you, splendid Lord of flame, bright, wonderful, Prince of men, is brought.
Offering with the sacred verse, Agni, bearer of our gifts.
Bring food to those who sing your praise.
6. These Agnis in the seats of fire nourish everything most excellent.
They give joy, they spread far and wide, they move constantly. Bring food to those who sing your praise.
7. Agni, these brilliant flames of yours grow like strong horses mightily,
Who with their hooves rush swiftly to the cattle stalls. Bring food to those who sing your praise.
8. To us who praise you, Agni, bring fresh food and safe and happy homes.
May we who have sung hymns to you have you as envoy in every house. Bring food to those who sing your praise.
9. You, brilliant God, within your mouth warmest both ladles of oil.
So fill us also, in our hymns, abundantly, Lord of Strength. Bring food to those who sing your praise.
10. Thus Agni have we honored with sacrifices and hymns.
May he give us what we desire, a store of brave sons and swift-footed steeds. Bring food to those who sing your praise.

HYMN VII. Agni.
URL: https://sacred-texts.com/hin/rigveda/rv05007.htm
--------------------------------------------------
Offer to Agni, dear friends, your proper food and proper praise;
To him supreme over all, the Strong One, mighty Lord:
When they gather in full assembly, rejoicing in his presence,
Even him worthy ones ignite, and living beings sustain.

When we present him with the food and offerings of men,
He grasps the holy ordinance with power and splendor.
He signals in the night to him far away,
Where the Bright One, unchanging through the ages, consumes the kings of the forest.

On him they place their high kin, as ridges on the land,
Whom many seek, mortal man finds to be the stay of all,
He who gives flavor to our food, the home of every living being.

As a herdsman cuts the grass, he tills the field and wilderness,
With flashing teeth and golden beard, deft with his unceasing might.

For him, as bright as an axe, he shines like Atri,
The well-bearer mother bears him, when her time comes due.
Bestow upon these mortals fame and splendor and intelligence.

Such zeal he has, unconquerable one: he won the cattle you gave.
Agni, may Atri overcome the Dasyus who do not give, subdue those who do not feed.

URL: https://sacred-texts.com/hin/rigveda/rv05008.htm
--------------------------------------------------
1. O Agni, eager for strength, the old ones who revered the Law, you were kindled for them,
 the Ancient, for their aid,
 You very bright, and holy, nourishing all, most excellent, the Friend and Master of the home.

2. You, Agni, men have established as their guest of old, as Master of the household, you, with hair of flame;
 High-bannered, multiform, distributing wealth, kind helper, good protector, drying the floods.

3. Tribes of men praise you, Agni, who well know burnt offerings, the Discriminator, lavish of wealth,
 Dwelling in secrecy, Blessed One! Visible to all, roaring loudly, skilled in worship, glorified with oil.

4. Ever to you, O Agni, as exceedingly strong, we draw near with songs and reverence, singing hymns.
 May you be pleased with us, Aṅgiras! As a God kindled by the noble with man’s goodly light.

5. You, Agni! Multiform, God much praised! Giving sustenance in every house as of old.
 You rule by your might over various foods: That light of yours, when blazing, may not be opposed.

6. The Gods, Most Youthful Agni, have made you, inflamed, the bearer of offerings and the messenger.
 You, widely-reaching, housed in sacred oil, invoked, brilliant, they have made the Eye stirring thoughts.

7. Men seeking joy have lit you, worshipped from old, O Agni, with good fuel and sacred oil.
 Thus, wet and growing mighty by plants, you spread yourself across the lands of earth.

URL: https://sacred-texts.com/hin/rigveda/rv05009.htm
--------------------------------------------------
1. BURNING AGNI, mortals offer you, O Agni, worship this divine one.
 I consider you Jātavedas: carry our offerings, ever unending.
 2 In the home where gifts are given, where grass is trimmed, Agni is the priest,
 To whom all sacrifices come and strengthenings that win renown.
 3 As a newborn baby, the kindling-sticks have brought to life,
 Sustainer of the human tribes, skilled in well-ordered sacrifice.
 4 Indeed, you are very hard to grasp, like the offspring of wriggling snakes,
 When you consume many forests like an ox, Agni, in the meadow.
 5 Whose flames, when you send up the smoke, reach the mark completely,
 When Tṛta in the height of heaven, like a smelting furnace, stirs you, like a smelting furnace sharpens you.
 6 O Agni, by your aid and Mitra’s friendly support,
 May we, avoiding hatred, subdue the wickedness of mortal men.
 7 O Agni, bring to our heroes such wealth, you victorious one.
 May he protect and nourish us, and help in gaining strength: be thou near us in battle for our success.

HYMN XI. Agni.
URL: https://sacred-texts.com/hin/rigveda/rv05011.htm
--------------------------------------------------
1. The powerful Agni, the protector of the people, has been born for new prosperity.

With oil on his face and a lofty flame, he shines brilliantly, pure, for the Bharatas.

2. Ensign of sacrifice, the earliest Household-Priest, the wise Priest has kindled Agni in his threefold seat,
With Indra and the Gods together on the grass, let the wise Priest sit to complete the sacrifice.

3. Pure, unadorned, from your two mothers you were born; you came as a charming sage from Vivasvan.
With oil they strengthened you, Agni, worshiped God; your banner was the smoke that rose to the sky.

4. May Agni graciously come to our sacrifice. The men carry Agni everywhere in every house.
He has become an envoy, bearer of our gifts; electing Agni, men choose one exceeding wise.

5. For you, O Agni, is this sweetest prayer of mine; dear to your spirit be this product of my thought.
As great streams fill the river, so our song of praise fills you, and makes you even mightier in your strength.

6. O Agni, the Aṅgirases discovered you when you were hidden, fleeing back from wood to wood.
You are produced through attrition as conquering might, and men, O Aṅgiras, call you the Son of Strength.

HYMN XII. Agni.
URL: https://sacred-texts.com/hin/rigveda/rv05012.htm
--------------------------------------------------
1. To Agni, noble Asura, worthy of worship, Guide of eternal Law, I offer my prayer;
 I sing my song, directed to the Mighty, like pure oil for his mouth at sacrifices.

2. Observe the Law, you who know it well; send forth the full streams of eternal Order.
 I use no magic with might or falsehood; I follow the sacred Law of the Red Steer.

3. How did you, follower of eternal Law, become the knower of a new song, Agni?
 The God, the Guardian of the seasons, knows me; the Lord of him who won this wealth I do not know.

4. Who, Agni, allied with your foe, what splendid helpers did they win for their riches?
 Agni, who guard the dwelling-place of falsehood? Who are the protectors of the speech of liars?

5. Those friends of yours have turned away from you; gracious once, they have become ungracious.
 They deceived themselves with their own words, uttering wicked things against the righteous.

6. He who pays sacrifice to you with honor, O Agni, keeps the Red Steer’s eternal Law;
 His dwelling is wide. May the noble offspring of Nahuṣa, who wandered forth, come here.

 You upheld the sky with a supporting pillar and spread the Earth, the Mother apart.
 3 You slew the flood-blocking serpent Vṛtra, Indra and Soma: Heaven approved your feat.
 You urged the river currents to flow swiftly and filled many seas with water.
 4 You set the rich milk in the unripe udders of the cows, Indra, and Soma.
 You held fast the unobstructed white within these many-colored living beings.
 5 Truly you bestow, Indra and Soma, wealth, famous, victorious, passing to our children.
 You endowed men, Mighty Beings, with manly strength that conquers in battle.

HYMN LXXIII. Bṛhaspati.
URL: https://sacred-texts.com/hin/rigveda/rv06073.htm
--------------------------------------------------
1. SERVED with offerings, first-born, mountain-born, Aṅgiras' son, Bṛhaspati, the Holy,
 With twice-strong path, dwelling in light, our Father, roars loudly, like a bull, to Earth and Heaven.

2. Bṛhaspati, who created for this people a wide space and border when the gods were invoked,
 Slashing through his enemies, he brings down their fortresses, subdues his foes, and overcomes those who hate him.

3. Bṛhaspati wins rich treasures in battle, wins this God great herds of cattle.
 Striving to win water and light, irresistible, Bṛhaspati strikes down the foe with lightning.

HYMN LXXIV. Soma-Rudra.
URL: https://sacred-texts.com/hin/rigveda/rv06074.htm
--------------------------------------------------
1. Hold your divine power firmly, O Soma-Rudra; may our offerings reach you swiftly.
 Place your seven great treasures in every home, bless our four-legged and two-legged beings.
 2 Soma and Rudra, chase away illness that has visited our homes.
 Drive Nirrti out into the distance, and grant us good fortune and happiness.
 3 Provide, O Soma-Rudra, all the necessary remedies for our bodies to heal and cure us.
 Release and draw away the sins we still carry within us.
 4 Armed with sharp arrows and weapons, kind and loving, be gracious to us, Soma and Rudra.
 Release us from Varuṇa's noose; keep us from sorrow, in your tender love.

HYMN LXXV. Weapons of War.
URL: https://sacred-texts.com/hin/rigveda/rv06075.htm
--------------------------------------------------
1. The warrior’s gaze is like a thundercloud, when clad in armor, he seeks the battlefield.
May you be victorious with an unharmed body; may your armor protect you thickly.

2. With the Bow, let us win cattle, with the Bow the battle, with the Bow let us be victors in our fierce encounters.
The Bow brings grief and sorrow to the enemy; armed with the Bow, may we subdue all regions.

3. Close to his chariot, she presses, holding her beloved close in embrace.
Strained on the Bow, she whispers like a woman—this Bowstring that preserves us in the fight.

4. These, meeting like a woman and her lover, bear, mother-like, their child upon their bosom.
May the two Bow-ends, starting swift apart, scatter, in unison, the foes who hate us.

5. With many sons, father of many daughters, He clangs and clashes as he goes to battle.
Slung on the back, pouring his offspring, the Quiver vanquishes all opposing bands and armies.

6. Upstanding in the Car, the skilled Charioteer guides his strong Horses wherever he wills.
See and admire the strength of those controlling reins which from behind declare the will of him who drives.

7. Horses whose hooves rain dust neigh loudly, yoked to the Chariots, showing forth their vigor,
With their forefeet descending on the enemy, they, never flinching, trample and destroy them.

8. Car-bearer is the name of his offering, whereon are laid his Weapons and his Armor.
So let us here, each day that passes, honor the helpful Car with hearts exceeding joy.

9. In sweet association lived the fathers who gave us life, profound and strong in trouble,
Unwearied, armed with shafts and wondrous weapons, free, real heroes, conquerors of armies.

10. The Brahmanas, and the Fathers meet for Soma-drinks, and, graciously inclined, unequalled Heaven and Earth.
Guard us from evil, Pūṣan, guard us, strengtheners of Law: let not the evil-wisher master us.

11. Her tooth a deer, dressed in an eagle's feathers, bound with cowhide, launched forth, She flies onward.
There where the heroes speed hither and thither, there may the Arrows shelter and protect us.
